### 📝 Introduction
> "A system is only as good as the plan behind it."

System Analysis and Design (SAD) is where technology meets business. This course taught me the methodologies used to analyze complex business problems and design effective information systems. For a Data Engineer, understanding the SDLC (Systems Development Life Cycle) is crucial because it ensures that the data architectures we build actually serve the users' needs and organizational goals.

---

## 📚 Syllabus & Topics
Based on the course modules, here is the breakdown of our focus areas:

| Topic | Topic Title | Key Concepts |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| 1️⃣ | **System Analysis Fundamentals** | Understanding roles, SDLC phases, and organizational impact. |
| 2️⃣ | **Project Planning Process** | Feasibility study, project scheduling, and resource management. |
| 3️⃣ | **Information Gathering** | Interactive (Interviews/JAD) and Unobtrusive methods. |
| 4️⃣ | **Data Flow Diagrams (DFD)** | Logical process modeling and drawing context diagrams. |
| 5️⃣ | **Process Specifications** | Structured decisions and formalizing system logic. |
| 6️⃣ | **ERD & Structure Charts** | Entity-Relationship modeling and modular system design. |
| 7️⃣ | **FR & NFR Requirements** | Functional vs. Non-Functional requirements (Security, UX, Performance). |

## 💭 Student Reflection

### 💡 Key Highlights
* **📋 Requirement Engineering:** I learned that the most important part of engineering isn't coding—it's asking the right questions. Learning how to gather requirements properly prevents building the "wrong" solution.
* **📐 Visual Modeling:** Tools like DFDs and ERDs make complex systems easy to visualize. It’s much easier to spot a logic flaw in a diagram than in 1,000 lines of code.

### 📈 Suggestions for Improvement
* *Modern Methodologies:* I would enjoy learning more about how these traditional SAD principles translate into modern **Agile** and **DevOps** environments used in the industry today.

### 🚀 Reflection
This course has shifted my perspective from being just a "coder" to being a "problem solver." As I look toward working in large-scale data environments, the ability to plan projects and design database relationships (ERDs) systematically will be one of my most valuable assets.
